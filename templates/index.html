7-stage pipeline · eligibility · keywords · semantic match · verdict · LaTeX rewriter

① eligibility gate
🛡️ Stage 1 — Hard Eligibility Gate
Checks instant disqualifiers: graduation window, visa sponsorship requirements, security clearance/citizenship, degree level (BS/MS/PhD), and location. A single FAIL = DO NOT APPLY regardless of keyword scores.
② parse quality
🔍 Stage 2 — Resume Parse Quality
Simulates ATS bots reading your PDF. Detects control characters, soft hyphens (U+00AD), broken words at line-ends, and multi-word phrase integrity. Score below 85 = ATS may misread your resume entirely.
③ required keywords
🔑 Stage 3 — Required Keyword Scoring
Extracts every required skill and qualification from the JD. Weighted scoring: technical skills (3), domain terms (2), soft skills (1). Heaviest factor — 35% of composite score.
④ preferred keywords
⭐ Stage 4 — Preferred Keyword Scoring
Scores "nice-to-have" qualifications. Missing them won't disqualify you, but hitting them pushes you to the top of the stack. 15% of composite score.
⑤ semantic match
🧠 Stage 5 — Semantic / Persona Match
Assesses holistic profile fit: career level, domain background, resume persona (analyst vs scientist vs engineer), overqualification/underqualification signals. 15% of composite.
⑥ recruiter review
👤 Stage 6 — Recruiter & Hiring Manager Review
Two-lens: Recruiter 6-second scan (name, school, degree, summary). Hiring manager 30-second read (project metrics, credibility). Together 20% of composite score.
⑦ composite score
📊 Stage 7 — Composite Score & Verdict
Parse 15% + Required 35% + Preferred 15% + Semantic 15% + Recruiter 10% + HM 10%. Verdict: 85+ = SUBMIT IMMEDIATELY · 75–84 = SUBMIT WITH EDITS · 65–74 = REVISE · below 65 = DO NOT APPLY.
