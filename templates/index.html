ATS Resume Analyzer

7-stage pipeline · contextual validation · adjacency matching · impact scoring · LaTeX rewriter

① eligibility gate
🛡️ Stage 1 — Hard Eligibility Gate
Checks instant disqualifiers: graduation window, visa sponsorship, clearance/citizenship, degree level, location. One FAIL = DO NOT APPLY regardless of all other scores.
② parse quality
🔍 Stage 2 — Resume Parse Quality
Rule-based ATS simulation: detects soft hyphens (U+00AD), control characters, broken multi-word phrases, missing detectable sections. Score below 80 = ATS likely misreads your resume.
③ required keywords
🔑 Stage 3 — Contextual Keyword Scoring
Extracts required skills from JD. Applies CONTEXT PENALTY: skills only in comma-separated lists score 0.5x. Skills proven in project bullet points score 1.0x. Weighted: technical (3x), domain (2x), soft (1x).
④ preferred keywords
⭐ Stage 4 — Adjacency Matching
Scores nice-to-have qualifications with semantic adjacency: XGBoost → gradient boosting (0.8x credit), Flask → REST API (0.8x credit). Missing them won't disqualify you but hitting them pushes you to the top.
⑤ semantic match
🧠 Stage 5 — Semantic Persona Match
Assesses holistic fit: career level alignment, domain depth, persona match (analyst vs scientist vs engineer), overqualification/underqualification signals. 15% of composite.
⑥ recruiter review
👤 Stage 6 — Impact Quantification
Counts bullet points WITH measurable metrics (numbers, %, scale) vs WITHOUT. Bullets without metrics score 0.7x. Identifies weakest bullets needing quantification. Recruiter + HM review = 20% of composite.
⑦ composite score
📊 Stage 7 — Composite Score & Verdict
Parse 15% + Required 35% + Preferred 15% + Semantic 15% + Recruiter 10% + HM 10%. Verdict: 85+ = SUBMIT IMMEDIATELY · 75–84 = SUBMIT WITH EDITS · 65–74 = REVISE SIGNIFICANTLY · below 65 = DO NOT APPLY.
